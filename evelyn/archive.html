About

Evelyn Hockstein is an award-winning photojournalist whose pictures compassionately and intimately capture the men, women, and children affected by war, poverty, environmental devastation, and political upheaval. She has worked in more than 60 countries raising awareness for humanitarian organizations and news outlets including The New York Times, Time, and Newsweek as well as CARE, UNHCR, and the World Food Program. Evelyn has won two Pictures of the Year International awards, the Days Japan International Photojournalism award, and has been nominated for a Pulitzer Prize. Her work has been exhibited in the US, UK, South Africa, Japan, and Kenya and her adventures have included traveling with Bill Clinton across Africa, climbing mountains in Pakistan for a story about the only female mountain rescue team, and scaling rigs in North Dakota for a reporting project on the oil rich Bakken. She is currently based in Washington, D.C.
